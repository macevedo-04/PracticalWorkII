What Is 3D Printing?

3D Printing is what is called an additive manufacturing process of making three-dimensional objects from a digital file. This method allows the creation of structures by superposing the material layer by layer.

3D Printing Applied To Aeronautics

3D Printing has spread throughout various fields, and the Space Industry is not an exception. Using this technique brings numerous advantages. For example, additive layer manufacturing enables us to build extremely complex pieces, otherwise impossible to create, and with the minimum material waste possible. This innovative method has brought numerous accomplishments by constructing thrusters, engines, and other fragments of spaceships and satellites. Not only that, it has even been able to print food in Space, and clothing for astronauts.

Surely, this is nothing but the begining of the implementation of 2D Printing in this outstanding field of work.
